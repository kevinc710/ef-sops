💸 Refund Policy (SOP 2)

Core rule non-negotiable

Refund only the discounted product subtotal — never the pre-discount price, never the total including shipping.

Example: Item $69.99 → Discount -$6.99 → Net paid $62.99. Refund = $62.99. Not $69.99. Not $67.99 (with shipping).

Steps to process a refund in Shopify

  1. Open the order → click Refund
  2. Select the correct quantity
  3. Leave shipping unchecked (do not refund shipping)
  4. Verify amount matches the discounted product subtotal
  5. Add internal note: "Refunded discounted product amount. Shipping not refunded per SOP."

Shipping refunds

🚨 Manager approval required — except when the wrong item was shipped or the order was confirmed lost due to company/carrier error.

The funds have been sent to your original payment method and should appear within 3-5 business days. Thanks for giving Ellison & Fitz a try. We hope to see you again. Best, The Ellison & Fitz Team

💳 Store Credit (SOP 1)

Eligibility — ALL must be true

  • Order is confirmed Delivered in carrier tracking
  • Customer agrees in writing to accept store credit
  • No active chargeback or payment dispute
  • Order is not flagged for fraud

Credit amount = product subtotal only

Include: item subtotal after discounts. Exclude: shipping, taxes, insurance/protection fees, payment processing fees.

How to issue in Shopify

  1. Go to Products → Gift Cards
  2. Click "Issue gift card"
  3. Enter exact product subtotal
  4. Associate with customer's email
  5. Add internal note: "Issued store credit for $[amount] instead of refund. Customer agreed via email on [DATE]. Credit for product subtotal only."

❌ Do NOT: create a discount code, process a manual refund, or use a draft order workaround.

Template — Store Credit Confirmation
Hi [Customer Name], Thanks for confirming. Your store credit in the amount of $[XX.XX] (representing the product subtotal) has now been issued to your account. This credit will be applied automatically to your next purchase and does not expire. We appreciate your understanding and look forward to seeing you again soon. Best, The Ellison & Fitz Team

📐 Sizing Guidance & Exchanges (SOP 6)

Step 1 — Pre-purchase sizing inquiries

  1. Refer the customer to the "Size Chart" link on the product page
  2. If no size chart is available → check with the supplier to verify sizing before responding. Do not guess.
  3. If customer is between sizes:
    • Recommend sizing UP for a more relaxed fit
    • Recommend sizing DOWN for a compression fit
    • If unsure which applies to that collection → check with the supplier for their recommendation before advising

Step 2 — Exchange requests

Eligibility: Item must be unworn, unwashed, and in original packaging.

  1. Confirm eligibility (30 days from delivery, unworn, original packaging)
  2. Direct the customer to the Redo portal to initiate the return
  3. Once the return is tracked in Redo → issue store credit or process the new size shipment
  4. Add Shopify internal note: "Sizing exchange processed from [Size] to [Size] via Redo portal."

Rules

✅
Be generous — especially with first-time customers
✅
Use "manufacturer sizing" for non-standard sizing — never "Chinese sizing"
✅
When in doubt on fit advice → check with supplier before responding
❌
Never blame the customer or say "you ordered the wrong size"
🚨
3rd exchange request or continued frustration → escalate to manager for full refund review

🏷️ Post-Purchase Discount Requests (SOP 3)

Core rule non-negotiable

Approved alternatives

2️⃣

Polite decline: If the request doesn't meet goodwill criteria.

🚨 High-Risk Refund (HRR) Protocol (SOP 4)

5 HRR triggers — initiate protocol if ANY apply

⚠️
Double Defect: Claiming a second defect on a replacement already sent for a previous defect
⚠️
Conditional Framing: "I'll remove my review if you refund me" or similar
⚠️
Repeated Behavior: 3+ refund/claim history from the same customer in 12 months
⚠️
Pressure Tactics: Threats of legal action, public complaints, or social media shaming
⚠️
Inconsistent Evidence: Photos/explanations that are contradictory or appear manipulated

5-Stage protocol

  1. Contain: Acknowledge and reassure — do not admit fault or promise an outcome
  2. Document: Tag order in Shopify (e.g., HRR-PressureTactics) and log in the Master Sheet
  3. Investigate: Collect order history, communications, evidence, tracking, delivery photos
  4. Decide: Apply the decision matrix below
  5. Log: Add detailed internal Shopify note and update Google Sheet with final resolution

Decision matrix

Double DefectClear photo/video evidence of both defectsApprove one final replacement (no refund)
Double DefectSuspiciousDecline

Repeated Behavior3+ claims in 12 monthsDecline; flag account; Escalate to manager
Pressure TacticsLegal threats or public shamingDecline while under duress; use containment template; Escalate immediately
Inconsistent EvidenceStory changes or evidence stagedDecline; request clearer info; Escalate if hostile

📦 Good Faith High-Value Dispute Protocol (SOP 7)

When this applies — ALL must be true

  • Customer claims an item is missing from their delivered package
  • Total order value is $100 or more
  • We have verified packing video evidence showing item was packed
  • Customer shows NO HRR triggers — if they do, switch to SOP 4

Resolution tiers

📦
Tier 1 ($100–$299): Send replacement of missing item + discount code. Do not require anything from customer.
🚨
Tier 2 ($300+): Escalate to Kevin or Leandro before communicating any resolution.

AI (Commslayer) rule

The AI agent is NOT authorized to resolve high-value missing item claims. If the order is $100+, Commslayer must hand off to human agent immediately. AI must not promise a replacement or refund.

Required documentation

  • Tag order: Missing-Item-Replaced
  • Add Shopify note with full summary of claim, video verification, and action taken
🚫
Never accuse the customer of lying. Frame video evidence as something reviewed internally — not as proof against them. Never send a replacement without first verifying the video.
📋 Unresponsive Customers & Damaged/Missing Claims (SOP 8) Updated May 28

Follow-up timeline

📅
Day 1: First outreach — ask for details or evidence needed
📅
Day 3 (no response): Send ONE polite follow-up reminder
📅
Day 7 (still no response): Close the ticket per closure rules below

Damaged / Missing / Incomplete shipment — investigation required

Core rule: Do not issue any refund, replacement, or resolution based on an email claim alone. Always go through the investigation process first.

Request from the customer:

  • Clear photos of the package exterior, including any damage
  • Photos of the shipping label
  • Photos of all sides of the packaging
  • Photos of all contents received

Closure rules

  • Do not auto-refund unless you literally cannot fulfill the order without the customer's response
  • If the customer placed an order and hasn't confirmed a requested change — ship what they originally ordered
  • Do not let urgency, threats, or pressure bypass the investigation process
  • If customer comes back after closure — reopen and handle normally
  • Mark ticket: "Closed - No Response"
Template — Damaged/Missing Shipment Investigation
Hi [Customer Name], Thank you for your follow-up. We understand your concern and take claims involving damaged, missing, or incomplete shipments seriously. However, we are unable to issue a refund solely based on an email claim without first completing our standard investigation process. At this time, we are reviewing the shipment details with our fulfillment and carrier records. To continue the investigation, please provide: • Clear photos of the package exterior, including any damage • Photos of the shipping label • Photos of all sides of the packaging • Photos of all contents received Once we receive this information, we will review the claim promptly and determine the appropriate resolution based on the findings. We appreciate your cooperation and patience while this process is completed. Best regards, Ellison & Fitz Support
